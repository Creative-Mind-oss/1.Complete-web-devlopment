Festivals of Neapal


Nepal, often called "The Land of Festivals", is a country rich in culture and traditions. With over 50 festivals celebrated every year From the grandeur of Dashain to the lights of Tihar, each festival brings people together, showcasing Nepal's unity in diversity.


Major Festivals of Nepal are listed below;


Festival Name Description
Dashain

Dashain is the longest and the most auspicious festival in the Nepalese annual calendar, celebrated by Nepalese people throughout the globe. It is a fifteen-day-long national festival of Nepal, which is the longest and the most anticipated festival in the country.

Key features:

  • Celebrates the victory of good over evil
  • Families reunite and receive blessings
  • Kite flying and animal sacrifices
Tihar

Tihar is a five-day-long Hindu festival celebrated in Nepal. It is the second biggest Nepalese festival after Dashain. It is considered to be of great importance as it shows reverence to not just the humans and the gods, but also to the animals like crows, cows, and dogs that maintain an intimate relationship with humans.

Key features:

  • Also known as the festival of lights
  • Each day is dedicated to different animals
  • Deusi Bhailo songs and dances
Indra Jatra

Indra Jatra is an eight-day-long festival celebrated by the Newar community in Kathmandu, Nepal. The festival is named after Lord Indra, the god of rain and heaven, and is celebrated to mark the end of the monsoon season.

Key features:

  • Living goddess Kumari is paraded through the streets
  • Masked dances and processions
  • Worship of the deity Akash Bhairav
